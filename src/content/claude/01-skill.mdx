<ConceptSection id="skill" term="Skill" category="claude" oneLiner="An encoded workflow that wears your taste.">

A **Skill** is a reusable workflow written as a markdown file that Claude reads and executes. It encodes your process — not just "do this task" but "do this task, the way I like it done, with these preferences and quality checks."

Skills are files in your project (or stored globally) with a `.md` extension. They describe steps, constraints, style rules, and expected outputs. When you trigger one, Claude reads the skill file first, then executes the workflow it describes.

<Diagram />

Examples of things people encode as skills: their code review process, their Figma-to-code workflow, their commit message format, their documentation style, their testing approach.

<Analogy>A saved Action in Photoshop — but for your entire design-to-development process. It runs your way, every time, without you re-explaining your preferences. The workflow has your taste baked in.</Analogy>

<WhyItMatters accent="var(--accent-claude)">Skills mean you stop re-explaining your process to Claude at the start of every session. Encode it once; it runs on demand. This is the difference between Claude as a tool you configure and Claude as a collaborator who already knows how you work.</WhyItMatters>
