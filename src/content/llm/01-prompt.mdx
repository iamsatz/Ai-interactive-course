<ConceptSection id="prompt" term="Prompt" category="llm" oneLiner="What you type. The brief.">

Every interaction with an AI model starts with a prompt. It is the full input you send — your question, instruction, or context. The model reads it, processes it, and generates a response based entirely on what you gave it.

A prompt can be a single sentence: *"Summarise this article."* Or it can be a multi-paragraph brief with context, constraints, examples, and desired output format. Both are prompts. The difference in quality between them is enormous.

<Diagram />

<Analogy>A creative brief to a designer — half the quality of the output is determined by the quality of the ask. A vague brief produces vague work. A precise brief with examples, constraints, and a clear goal produces precise work.</Analogy>

<WhyItMatters accent="var(--accent-llm)">Prompting is a design skill, not a technical one. The model is capped by how clearly you articulate the problem. You do not need to know how the model works to write good prompts — you need to think clearly about what you want.</WhyItMatters>
