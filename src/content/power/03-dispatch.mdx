<ConceptSection id="dispatch" term="Dispatch / Cowork" category="power" oneLiner="Long-running async agents. Claude that works while you sleep.">

**Dispatch** (sometimes called Cowork) is the mode where you hand Claude a long, complex task and let it run autonomously for an extended period — minutes to hours — without interactive supervision.

You provide a detailed brief: the goal, constraints, what "done" looks like, what to avoid. Claude works through it in the background, using the agent loop to iterate. You come back to results.

<Diagram />

The key difference from a regular session: in a regular session, Claude waits for you after each step. In Dispatch, it keeps going. You review the output at the end, not at every step.

Good Dispatch tasks: codebase audits, migrating a design system, writing documentation, refactoring a large module, research synthesis. Bad Dispatch tasks: anything where early decisions need human input to stay on track.

<Analogy>Leaving your junior a detailed brief before going to bed. It's done when you wake up — but only if the brief was clear enough to cover every decision they'd otherwise have to ask you about.</Analogy>

<WhyItMatters accent="var(--accent-power)">Useful for tasks that take hours. Instead of a 3-hour interactive session where you review every step, you write a thorough brief once and get a complete result. Time-multiplies your output.</WhyItMatters>
