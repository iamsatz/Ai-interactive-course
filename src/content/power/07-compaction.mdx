<ConceptSection id="compaction" term="Compaction" category="power" oneLiner="Claude summarizes its own history when context gets long. You can also trigger it manually.">

Every Claude Code session has a context window — finite tokens. When you fill it, older messages get pushed out and the model starts forgetting things. Compaction is the fix: Claude summarizes earlier parts of the conversation into a shorter form, freeing room for new work.

It happens two ways:

**Automatic** — Claude Code triggers compaction when context fills past a threshold. You'll see a notice; the session continues with a summarized history.

**Manual** — Run `/compact` whenever you feel the session getting heavy. Don't wait for auto-compaction. Triggering early gives you cleaner context for the next chunk of work.

<Diagram />

What survives compaction: major decisions, file paths you've been editing, your CLAUDE.md, anything Claude explicitly tagged as important. What gets summarized: search noise, intermediate tool outputs, exploratory back-and-forth that's no longer relevant.

<Analogy>Clearing your desk halfway through a long project. Sketches go in the bin. Final notes get pinned to the board. You keep moving with the things that still matter.</Analogy>

<WhyItMatters accent="var(--accent-power)">Long sessions without compaction degrade. The model starts contradicting itself, forgetting your constraints, repeating earlier steps. Manual compaction at the right moment — before a heavy task, after finishing a sub-goal — keeps the session sharp.</WhyItMatters>
