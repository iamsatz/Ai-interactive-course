<ConceptSection id="diff" term="Diff" category="git" oneLiner="The red/green view of exactly what changed.">

A **diff** shows the precise difference between two versions of a file. Red lines were removed. Green lines were added. Lines with neither colour are context — unchanged surrounding lines included so you understand where the change lives.

Claude Code shows you a diff before applying most changes. This is intentional. The diff is your review gate — the moment to check that what the model is proposing matches what you actually wanted.

<Diagram />

Reading diffs gets faster with practice. Focus on:
- The **scope** — how many files changed?
- The **nature** — is it adding logic, restructuring, or deleting something?
- **Unexpected lines** — anything that wasn't what you asked for

If the diff looks right, approve it. If anything is unexpected, reject it and clarify your prompt.

<Analogy>Track Changes in a Google Doc — but for code. A transparent record of exactly what changed between two states. Claude shows the diff before applying anything. This is your one review step before changes land.</Analogy>

<WhyItMatters accent="var(--accent-git)">Always read the diff before accepting. This is your review step. Do not skip it. Every time you approve a diff without reading it, you are giving up oversight of your codebase. This is especially true for AI-generated changes.</WhyItMatters>
